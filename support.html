Grant Application Steps

2-3 Months Before Screening Events

  1. Email faculty mentors at Baylor College of Medicine (BCM) and McGovern Medical School. Previous mentors include Drs. Dana Clark, Ravi Chundru, and Andrew Lee.
  2. Email site mentors to confirm support and dates for health fairs. Previous health fair sites include:
    • Open Gate Homeless Ministries. Contact: Damien Kelly (damienkel@gmail.com)
    • Casa Maria. Contact: Juliana Zapata or Monse (casamaria6101@gmail.com)
    • Día De La Mujer Latina. Contact: Venus Gines (venusgines@gmail.com)
    • HOMES Clinic. Contacts: Dana Clark (dlclark@bcm.edu) and Ravi Chundru (rkc@rkcmd.com)
  3. Email Prevent Blindness Texas to confirm they have vision screening machines available on the days of the health fairs. Confirm they have a vision screener volunteer to operate the vision screening machine.
    • Contacts: Monica Guerrero (mguerrero@pbtexas.org) and Tiffany Ramos (tramos@pbtexas.org)
  4. Write a draft of the grant! Make sure to follow the guidelines and address each section. Examples are stored in the Google Drive. Previous grants that this initiative applied to include:
    • BCM Gold Humanism Honors Society Service Project Grant
    • Gold Summer Student Fellowships
  5. Reach out to BCM/McGovern deans of student affairs to gain a letter of support. They need to state that you are in good academic standing, express support for the choice of faculty mentors and site locations, and declare that the school is willing to accept the funds if the applicants are awarded. Send them a draft of the grant early because deans can get busy!
  6. Fill out Baylor's online Biomedical Research and Assurance Information Network (BRAIN) at least 10 days before submission is due! It's better to start early because approval can take a lot of time.

1 Month Before Screening Events

  1. Email BCM Office of Graduate Medical Education (GME) about funding mechanisms once you hear back from a successful grant application. Contacts include: Caroline Hubbard (caroline.hubbard@bcm.edu), Jonae Boatwright (jonae.boatwright@bcm.edu), and Ivan Navarrete (ivan.navarrete@bcm.edu).
  2. Order reading glasses and sunglasses! Previous supplies include:
    • Reading Glasses & Cases
    • Sunglasses
  3. Email partner health fairs and Prevent Blindness Texas to re-confirm dates and vision screening machine availability.

2-3 Weeks Before Screening Events

  1. Make flyers for the volunteer events.
  2. Create a volunteer sign up sheet in Google Forms. Include half volunteers from BCM and half from McGovern. Encourage at least one older student (MS3's & MS4's) to encourage peer-guided support and learninf for the younger students (MS1's & MS2's).
  3. Post the flyers and volunteer sign ups on BCM and McGovern ophthalmology interest group social medias and email groups. Tag the Gold Foundation.
  4. For HOMES Clinic specifically, make sure BCM students fill out their NICER authorization forms for consent.

1-2 Weeks Before Screening Events

  1. Print flyers, Prevent Blindess Texas vision screening forms, vision test charts, photo release forms, and list of partnering clinics (Found in Google Drive)
  2. Hang up flyers in clinc (1 week before).
  3. Email volunteers vision screening tutorials and link to this website.
  4. Print a volunteer sign-in sheet to help track hours.
  5. Send a reminder email to volunteers and Prevent Blindness Texas to confirm availability (1 week before).

2-3 Days Before Screening Events

  1. Organize boxes containing reading glasses and sunglasses. Make sure to label perscription of glasses on the box.
  2. Post again on BCM and McGovern social medias. Tag the Gold Foundation.

Post-Event

  1. Scan Prevent Blindness Texas vision screening forms and make a PDF copy. Censor all PHI (name, date of birth).
  2. Enter data into the shared excel doc to track demographics, barriers to care, visual acuity, and improvement after donation of glasses.
  3. Contact volunteers to gather feedback for how to improve future events.
  4. Email Gold Humanism monthly progress reports about screenings, including data collected and photos with photo release forms.
